# AI Hedge Fund — Autonomous Multi-Agent Equity Research System

An advanced AI-powered equity research platform that simulates an institutional-grade investment workflow using specialized autonomous agents.

The system integrates fundamental analysis, technical signals, sentiment intelligence, and adversarial risk evaluation into a unified decision engine that produces high-conviction, risk-aware trade recommendations.

---

## Core Philosophy

Traditional investing tools provide fragmented insights.

This system replaces that with a coordinated multi-agent architecture where each agent has a well-defined responsibility. Decisions are made through structured analysis, cross-validation, and adversarial testing before final execution.

---

## System Architecture (Agent-Based)

### 1. Quantitative Agent (Data-Driven Analyst)

Purpose: Extract structured financial intelligence

* Parses financial statements (Balance Sheet, Income Statement, Cash Flow)
* Computes key financial ratios such as ROE, ROCE, Debt/Equity, Free Cash Flow
* Performs growth analysis, trend detection, and historical comparisons
* Outputs a data-driven financial health score

---

### 2. Qualitative Agent (Narrative Intelligence Engine)

Purpose: Interpret business quality beyond numbers

* Processes annual reports, earnings call transcripts, and investor presentations
* Extracts management tone, strategy, and long-term direction
* Detects language shifts across years and hidden concerns in disclosures
* Outputs contextual understanding of business quality

---

### 3. Technical Agent (Market Behavior Analyst)

Purpose: Analyze price action and timing

* Evaluates price trends, volume behavior, and market structure
* Uses indicators such as Moving Averages, RSI, MACD, and support/resistance
* Identifies entry/exit zones, breakouts, and reversals
* Outputs timing and momentum signals

---

### 4. Sentiment & News Agent (Real-Time Intelligence Layer)

Purpose: Capture real-time market perception

* Scans financial news and headlines continuously
* Performs sentiment classification (bullish, bearish, neutral)
* Detects major events such as earnings, regulations, or macro shocks
* Tracks sudden sentiment shifts
* Outputs a market sentiment score

---

### 5. Supply Chain & Competitive Intelligence Agent

Purpose: Understand real-world business exposure

* Identifies suppliers, customers, and ecosystem relationships
* Uses trade data, job postings, and disclosures to reconstruct networks
* Performs supply chain mapping and competitor benchmarking
* Simulates disruptions (e.g., geopolitical risks, natural disasters)
* Outputs revenue exposure risk and competitive positioning insights

---

### 6. Risk & Red Flag Detection Agent

Purpose: Detect hidden risks from unstructured data

* Continuously scans filings, disclosures, and legal information
* Detects new or removed risk factors
* Identifies litigation, regulatory pressure, and related-party transactions
* Extracts debt covenants and contractual risks
* Outputs structured risk alerts

---

### 7. Devil’s Advocate Agent (Portfolio Protector)

Purpose: Reject weak investment ideas

This agent acts as the final defense layer and does not support trades. Its role is to challenge and invalidate assumptions.

* Questions every investment thesis
* Searches for historical failure patterns and red flags
* Identifies overvaluation, weak narratives, and inconsistencies
* Stress-tests assumptions from all other agents
* Has veto power over trade decisions

---

### 8. Portfolio Manager Agent (Decision Engine)

Purpose: Generate final actionable decisions

* Aggregates outputs from all agents
* Applies portfolio constraints and risk management rules
* Produces final recommendation: BUY, HOLD, or SELL
* Generates confidence score and structured reasoning

---

## Decision Flow

Raw Data → Agents Process Independently → Insights Aggregated
→ Devil’s Advocate Challenges Thesis
→ Portfolio Manager Final Decision

---

## Human-Like Reasoning Layer

This system mimics how professional investment teams operate:

* Multiple independent perspectives
* Structured internal debate
* Skepticism-driven validation
* Context-aware reasoning instead of purely numerical outputs
